Executive Summary

Revenue grew 6.5% year-over-year. That headline hides six findings, ranked here by dollar exposure:

1

$1.95M is sitting in excess inventory Critical

261 of 403 stocked SKUs (65%) hold more than 90 days of cover — together 95% of total stock value. More than half of one year's revenue is sitting on shelves instead of circulating as cash.

$1.95M
2

Quiet decline outweighs outright churn 12-to-1 Critical

90 customers stayed active but shrank their spend, losing $759K, compared with $61K lost to the 121 customers who churned completely. Decline among still-active accounts costs twelve times more than outright churn, and it needs account-level attention rather than a win-back campaign.

−$759K
3

Sindh is shrinking while carrying 64% of revenue Warning

The largest region declined 5.5% year-over-year even as smaller regions grew 20–76%. A flat national number is masking an erosion in the core market.

−5.5%
4

Two accounts explain 44% of all customer-level revenue decline Warning

The two largest declining accounts (−$299K and −$137K) together account for $436K of the $759K quiet-decline total, so direct outreach on just these two accounts would meaningfully move the number.

−$436K
5

18.9% of SKUs drive 80% of revenue Structural

Classic long-tail: 245 SKUs (58%) contribute only 5% of revenue. A rationalization pass on the C-class tail would simplify operations with minimal revenue risk.

Opportunity
6

One sales officer drives 28% of two-year revenue Warning

Officer 12 alone generated $2.25M. A second officer's $500K book sits entirely on a single customer. Both are key-person concentration risks worth formal succession planning.

Methodology & Notes

  • Source: two sales extracts (2024–2025 full year; Jan–Feb 2026 partial) totaling 26,411 transaction line-items, plus a point-in-time stock export (403 SKUs, matched to sales via normalized product code).
  • Currency converted at a fixed 280 PKR/USD rate for illustration; does not reflect exchange-rate movement over the period.
  • Inventory value is estimated — stock quantity × trailing average selling price per SKU (85% of stocked SKUs matched to a sales-derived price; the remainder have no recent price reference).
  • "Days of Cover" = stock quantity ÷ (trailing-12-month sales quantity ÷ 365). SKUs with zero L12M sales are flagged "No Sales History" rather than assigned a cover figure.
  • 2026 figures reflect January–February only and are not a full-year comparison.
  • SKU counts differ by scope: 596 is the full historical product catalog; 424 SKUs have sales history and are ABC-classified (Class A 80 + B 99 + C 245); 403 SKUs appear in the point-in-time stock export used for the inventory-risk section.
  • Revenue figures are rounded to two decimals for display (e.g. $3.59M, $3.83M); growth percentages (e.g. +6.5% YoY) are computed from the unrounded underlying totals, so they won't recompute exactly from the rounded figures shown.
  • All customer, officer, and warehouse identities are anonymized (Client N / Officer N); product identities use SKU codes only.

Analysis built on the underlying Power BI semantic model (star schema, 50+ DAX measures). Figures here are independently cross-validated against the dashboard's live measures.
